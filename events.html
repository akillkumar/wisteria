Dance your heart out in styles such as hip hop, breaking, freestyle, locking, popping. Come show us your passion for dance as you jump, flip, and groove to victory in this group event!

Date: 24 February
Time: 10:30am - 01:30pm (3 hours)
Venue: Basketball Court


Rules

Team Size: 5-18
Teams per College: 2
Registration Fee: INR 500
Styles: The choreography performed must be within the western dance sphere, with forms such as hip hop, popping, locking, freestyle, tutting, krumping, breaking, wacking.

Preliminary Round (Online)
  • • All teams registering will be required to submit a 3-5 minute video of their dance.
  • • The video must be submitted in .mp4 format.
  • • The video must be mailed to banjaara.events@ashoka.edu.in by 14th February, 2019. The subject line of this email should be ‘Prelims- Street Dance’. The results will be announced by 20th February.
  • • The body of the email must consist of the team name, name and number of members, and institution name. The video should consist of whichever part of the dance the performers find most suitable for auditions. Performing in full costume and with props is preferred.

  • • Only 2 teams per college are allowed to participate.
  • • The strength of a team should be between 5 to 18 members.
  • • Selected teams will be required to perform their full choreography of 6-8 minutes, with 2 extra minutes for setup.
  • • All teams selected must submit their audio tracks (mp3 format) in advance via mail and must carry them on a pendrive on the day of the competition as well.
  • • All props used need to be informed about in advance. Participants must bring their own props, the university will not provide any. Hazardous props like flammables and sharp objects will not be allowed.
  • • The time limit as clearly stated above must be kept in mind, exceeding them will lead to deduction of points.
  • • There is no bar on the language of songs used. Participants are free to use as many songs as they want, they can also mix songs.
  • • The judges’ decision will be final and abiding.

  • • Costume
  • • Creativity
  • • Musicality
  • • Energy
  • • Stage/Space utilization
  • • Overall impact

Battle of Bands
  • Details
  • Judging Criteria

This competition will have different bands battle it out to see who can play the best music. Singers, guitarist, pianists, percussionists, this event is for all of you!

Date: 24 February
Time: 01:30pm - 04:30pm
Venue: Main Stage


Rules

Team Size: 3-8
Teams per College: 2 Finalists from each college (as many can apply for prelims)
Registration Fee: INR 800

Preliminary Round (Online)
  • • The prelims will be judged internally through 4-5 minute video clips.
  • • The video must include all members of the group.
  • • Audio may be recorded into a software and the volume levels adjusted with the faders. No effects will be allowed post recording (EQ, delay etc.).
  • • The video and audio quality should be of a decent level in order to facilitate a smoother judging procedure. Entries with below-average quality stand the chance of not being considered.
  • • The videos must be sent to the email ID banjaara.events@ashoka.edu.in. The subject line must be ‘Prelims- BoB’. The body of the email must consist of the band name, institution name, and the number and names of all team members.
  • • The last date for submission of prelim video clips will be 14th February, 2019. Results will be announced by 20th February.

General Rules
  • • There is no restriction on the languages used.
  • • A vocalist is compulsory.
  • • No backing tracks are allowed.
  • • Any pre-recorded material/MIDI samples/sequencing will lead to direct disqualification.
  • • Each team will get a maximum of 25 minutes to perform. This includes set-up time. Extra time taken will result in a deduction of points in 30-second intervals, corresponding to the lower boundary exceeded (ie. if a team goes one second over 25 minutes, points are deducted. Additionally, if the team goes a second above 1 minute, another set of points will be deducted).
  • • In case the band is performing an original composition, a document with the lyrics must be sent to banjaara.events@ashoka.edu.in after registration with the subject line ‘BoB - Original Lyrics’. The email must mention the name of the institute, name of the band (if any), names of band members, and name of the song.
  • • If a song is in a language other than English or Hindi, a document with the literal translation should be sent to banjaara.events@ashoka.edu.in with the subject line ‘BoB - Translation’. The email must mention the name of the institute, name of the band (if any), names of band members, song name and language of the song.
  • • The judges’ decision will be final and abiding.

Judging Criteria
  • • Pitch
  • • Harmony
  • • Syncronisation
  • • Technicality
  • • Stage Performance
  • • Creative interpretation- covers of songs reimagined creatively, improvisation, etc. will be appreciated

Sold!

This IPL auction simulation will keep you on the edge of your seat as you decide which players to bid on to form a team of players that have the highest cumulative base price.

Date: 24 February
Time: 11:00am - 02:00pm
Venue: Classroom


Rules

Team Size: 1-4
Teams per College: No bar
Registration Fee: INR 300 for solo team, INR 600 per team

General Rules
  • • Cross-college teams are allowed.
  • • A player will be projected on the screen for five minutes before the auction starts. The list of players will be divided into wicket-keepers, batsmen, bowlers and all-rounders
  • • Teams will be allotted Rs 120 Cr to buy the respective players.
  • • Player auctions will be done by giving base prices, which will increase sequentially.
  • • Each team has to have a minimum of 16 players, where at least 10 have to be Indian nationals.
  • • Team rosters must contain 2 wicket-keepers, 5 batsmen, 5 bowlers, and 4 all-rounders.
  • • Failing to comply with the previous 2 points will result in disqualification.
  • • After bidding for the last player has been done, the unsold players of the auction will be put up for sale at twice their respective base price.
  • • After the auction has been completed, the floor will be opened for trades between franchises. Each franchise is allowed to do 2 trades only. The trades have to be direct swaps, and no monetary transactions will be allowed.
  • • At the end of the trading period, the team with the highest cumulative base price of all players will be declared the winners.


Judging Criteria

Highest cumulative base price

Khayal
  • Details
  • Judging Criteria

A 3 round english short story/essay writing competition. Participants will have to write an original piece in third person point of view while incorporating the given elements at different rounds. A suitable title to be given.

Date: 23 February
Time: 11:00am - 01:00pm
Venue: Classroom (TBD)


Rules

Team Size: Individual participants
Teams per College: No cap
Registration Fee: INR 200 per participant

General Rules
  • • This is a creative writing competition that tests your imagination and writing skills in the english language.
  • • There will be two rounds. In the first round, participants must write 500 word short stories by tying together three different elements of a story (for example, plot point, character, location).
  • • Each element will be decided by a wheel. Contestants will spin each wheel, and must concoct a story with the three options provided by the wheel.
  • • The second round gives participants a chance to extend their story by 200-300 words by spinning a fourth wheel, and adding it to the existing story.
  • • Each participant will have 5 minutes to think before each round, and each round will be 30 minutes long.
  • • All stories must be written in English and submitted online (detailed instructions regarding submission process shall be communicated to the participants on the day of the event). Participants are requested to carry/arrange for their laptops and chargers to the event. The university shall not be providing any devices under any circumstance.
  • • The judges’ decision will be final and abiding.


Judging Criteria
  • • Originality
  • • Creativity
  • • Incorporation of prompts
  • • Language, vocabulary and grammar
  • • Writing style
  • • Story build up
  • • Consistency

It's not easy to make a film. Anyone who has ever tried can attest to the many hurdles that must be leapt over. However, it's these unique challenges which one must face that give rise to the most creative solutions - and the most rewarding of experiences. We, at AFF, want to help promote this resourceful, talented and passionate lot of student filmmakers all over the country. So, grab a camera and let's go!

Date: 24 February
Time: 12:00pm - 01:30pm
Venue: Classroom (TBD)


Rules

Team Size: No bar
Teams per College: No bar
Registration Fee: INR 350 per film entry

General Rules
  • • This competition is open to anyone currently enrolled at an undergraduate programme anywhere in India. There is no age bar.
  • • There may be any number of members in a team but the credits must clearly state the director(s).
  • • Participants may submit any number of entries. This year, we are not accepting animated films.
  • • Films may be in any and as many languages but they should all be accompanied with English subtitles, including English films.
  • • Films must adhere to a strict maximum runtime of ten minutes (including title and credits).
  • • Works with adult subject matter is welcome; Ashoka Film Festival (AFF) does not impose any “PG”/ “U/A” standard restriction. However, blatant obscenity will not be entertained.
  • • Films are required to use original or music and sound or license it. Music without copyright and covers of songs are also acceptable.
  • • Films must be original stories or original interpretations of stories.
  • • Films that have been submitted to other festivals are allowed.
  • • Films submitted must have been made within the last one year (January 1st, 2018 onwards).
  • • Films must be submitted latest by 14th February 2019. There is no restriction of theme.
  • • Submissions must be made to banjaara.events@ashoka.edu.in in mp4 format with the subject line ‘Short Film Submission’, and should be accompanied with the names of all those involved, and the email address and phone number of a designated person of contact.
  • • Selected films will be screened at the Ashoka Film Festival, which is part of Banjaara 2019. Those selected will be notified. Prizes will be distributed on these days only.
  • • Films should be submitted in at least 720p picture quality (HD).
  • • AFF reserves the right to publish whole or parts of submitted films on YouTube/Instagram/Facebook for promotional purposes. We do not claim ownership. These will not be monetized.


Judging Criteria
  • • Content
    • • Story
    • • Structure
    • • Originality
  • • Technical expertise
    • • Cinematography
    • • Production design
    • • Sound
    • • Editing
  • • Overall impact and quality of content
